27 Nov 2025

Chinese #RhodesScholar Tony Wang reflects upon what it means to have had ten years of the #RhodesScholarship in China.

Tony is in the first term of his DPhil (PhD) in Archaeology at Oxford. His academic passion lies in the history and archaeology of the Silk Roads, seeking to reconstruct the history of Central Asia through an interdisciplinary lens.

26 Nov 2025

As we celebrate ten years of the #RhodesScholarship in China, Ryan Yan, a 2020 #RhodesScholar shares the path her life has taken since first hearing about the #RhodesScholarship as an undergraduate:

"I was tempted by the Scholarship but initially it felt out of reach. Hearing fellow Nanjing University alumna, Yan Chen, share her experiences acted as a spark of encouragement to apply but I didnt expect it to open such a wonderful chapter.

"At Oxford, I cooked curry with Indian classmates, discussed Asian literature and queer theory with American Scholars, and played erhu accompanied by a Syrian Rhodes Scholar on classical guitar. These beautiful moments deeply moved me and expanded my imagination of lifes possibilities.

"This intercultural dialogue not only enriched my life, but helped me to think from an interdisciplinary perspective, encountering research methods from diverse fields like economics and computer science. My experiences at Oxford gave me solid academic quality and a network, making me more confident in facing challenges."

Now a Ph.D. student at Stanford University Ryan studies emotion using neuroimaging and computational methods to investigate the interplay between emotions and decision-making, and how these processes might break down in mood disorders. These findings may not be clinically applicable yet, but understanding the mechanisms of mood disorders through the lens of reward and punishment can help lay the scientific foundation for more effective diagnosis and treatment.

20 Nov 2025

Chrystia Freeland has been appointed as the next Warden of Rhodes House and Chief Executive Officer of the Rhodes Trust. She will start in her new role on 1 July 2026.

An Alberta-born #RhodesScholar who came to Oxford in 1991, Chrystia brings a remarkable record of achievement as a prominent Canadian politician, acclaimed journalist, and award-winning author. Amongst other positions she served as Canadas Deputy Prime Minister and has been recognised for her significant contributions to public life. She says:

"It is an honour and privilege to return to Oxford as Warden of Rhodes House and CEO of the Rhodes Trust. Having personally benefitted from a Rhodes Scholarship, I know how deeply transformative and influential it can be on the lives and futures of our Scholars. The experience helped shape my international outlook and played a defining role in guiding my subsequent career. I look forward to working with the Trust to build on its remarkable legacy and further strengthen the impact of the Scholarships on individual Scholars, on the global Rhodes community, and on society."

19 Nov 2025

"I was very involved with the Oxford University Gliding Club. I spent more weekends at Weston learned to fly having had no prior experience, and got my sailplane pilots licence! While understanding of physics of flying came relatively naturally to me, I had a magnificent amount of fun, learned so much about decision making, self-reliance and team work. I made some amazing friends from all walks of life. Gliding was something that wasnt in my consciousness at all before I came to Oxford."

#RhodesScholar Siobhan Tobin reflects upon the intersections between academic study, extracurricular pursuits and self-development during her DPhil (PhD) in Physics at Oxford. The #RhodesScholarship enables and encourages Scholars to enjoy a well-rounded experience at Oxford, where time in the sky can be as educational as time in the lab!

14 Nov 2025

For our December Scholars' Library virtual event, #RhodesScholar Dr Joy Buolamwini, in conversation with fellow Scholar Meredith Ross-James, will discuss her book Unmasking AI: My Mission to Protect What Is Human in a World of Machine.

Dr Joy Buolamwini, founder of the Algorithmic Justice League, stands at the vanguard of AI ethics as a pioneering MIT researcher and multidisciplinary artist. Her landmark Gender Shades research galvanized the field of algorithmic auditing, ranking among the most influential AI bias studies ever published. This consequential research compelled three tech giants to halt sales of facial recognition technologies (FRTs) to law enforcement and sparked worldwide discourse on ethical AI implementation.

Meredith Ross-James is a philosopher and activist currently reading for the Bachelor of Philosophy at Oxford. Her research interests include the philosophy of conspiracy theories, political extremism studies, political theory, social epistemology, and ethics.

The Scholars Library is a monthly book talk series where Rhodes alumni can come together to present, discover and debate their literary works.
