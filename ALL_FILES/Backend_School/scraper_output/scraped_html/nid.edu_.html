National Institute of Design (NID) is internationally acclaimed as one of the finest educational and research institutions for Industrial, Communication, Textile and IT Integrated (Experiential) Design. It is a statutory institute under the DPIIT, Ministry of Commerce and Industry, Government of India. It has been recognised as an 'Institution of National Importance' by the Act of Parliament, by virtue of the National Institute of Design Act 2014. It is also recognised by the Department of Scientific and Industrial Research, Government of India as a Scientific and Industrial Research Organisation.

National Institute of Design, Ahmedabad Hosts Design Research Symposium Showcasing 23 Scholars

The National Institute of Design (NID), Ahmedabad successfully hosted its Design Research Symposium 2025 – Innovative Enquiries in Doctoral Research on 13th and 14th November, 2025. A total of 23 doctoral scholars from NID presented their ongoing and completed research work through papers, highlighting diverse perspectives across design disciplines.

Group Captain Shri Shubhanshu Shukla Visited the National Institute of Design

Group Captain Shri Shubhanshu Shukla, a distinguished test pilot of the Indian Air Force and the first Indian astronaut to visit the International Space Station (ISS), visited the National Institute of Design (NID) Ahmedabad & Gandhinagar on the 10th & 11th of November, 2025.

National Institute of Design, Ahmedabad signs MoU with Prime Ministers’ Museum and Library.

Hon’ble Prime Minister of India has given a clarion call for “Design in India, Design for the World.” In alignment with this visionary call and to translate the Hon’ble Prime Minister’s vision of making Bharat Atmanirbhar in the field of design, the National Institute of Design (NID), Ahmedabad, under the leadership of Dr. Ashok Mondal, Director, has initiated several strategic collaborations with government ministries and institutions for promoting design-led research and innovation.

The identity of this book edited by the late I.S. Mathur is established by its leitmotif—training the teachers of design or educating the design educators. This book is divided into three major sections—‘Retrospection’, ‘Introspection’, and ‘Perception’. A mélange of thoughts, experiences, and anecdotes expressed by alumni, faculty both former and present, and artists—this book touches upon poignant issues which can influence the spread of design education across design schools/institutions in India.
